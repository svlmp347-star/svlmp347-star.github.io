GitHub Website

I am a science student from rural India working at the intersection of biological systems, artificial intelligence, and technology-driven public infrastructure. My interests are shaped by questions of scale: how complex systems—biological or institutional—fail, and how they can be redesigned using scientific and engineering principles.

I am particularly drawn to problems where theory must survive real-world constraints, whether in neuroscience, healthcare delivery, or embedded systems deployed at population scale.

Intellectual Interests

  • Neurodegeneration and systems-level neuroscience
  • Artificial intelligence for medical and biological data
  • Embedded and cyber-physical systems
  • Technology for public infrastructure and health systems
  • Translational research: from theory to deployment

Selected Work

Smart Grain ATM

RFID-authenticated, sensor-driven grain distribution system

Designed and implemented an automated grain dispensing system intended to improve transparency and accuracy in public food distribution. The system integrates embedded hardware, secure authentication, precise weight sensing, and cloud-based logging.

The core challenge was translating a socio-economic problem into a reliable cyber-physical system under real-world constraints, balancing cost, robustness, and accountability.
